Semantic HTML: The Flawless Silhouette

A truly iconic look isn't just about the flashy accessories; it's about the structure and the perfect fit underneath. Semantic HTML gives your webpage a flawless silhouette, making sure everything is where it's supposed to be. It's not just for looks—it helps browsers and search engines understand your vibe.

The Main Layout: Your Head-to-Toe Look

Using the right tags for the right parts of your page is non-negotiable. It's the difference between a custom-fit design and a fashion disaster.

  • <header>: The top of your look. Usually has the main title and logo.
  • <nav>: Your navigation bar. It's like the map to your entire collection.
  • <main>: The star of the show! This is where the main, unique content of the page goes.
  • <footer>: The bottom of the page. Perfect for copyright info or contact links.

<body>
  <header>
    <h1>My Glam Site</h1>
  </header>
  <nav>
    <a href="/">Home</a>
    <a href="/looks">Looks</a>
  </nav>
  <main>
    <p>The main content goes here!</p>
  </main>
  <footer>
    <p>© 2025 Glam-Stack</p>
  </footer>
</body>

The Footer: The Perfect Shoes

A footer that floats in the middle of the page is a total faux pas. To make sure your footer always stays at the bottom—like the perfect shoes completing the look—you can use this simple CSS trick with Flexbox.


body {
  min-height: 100vh;
  display: flex;
  flex-direction: column;
}

footer {
  margin-top: auto;
}

JavaScript Modules: Your Style Kit

You need to link your JavaScript to your HTML to make your page interactive. There are two ways to bring in your style kit.

The Classic Script

This is the old-school way. It just runs the script file.


<script src="/assets/js/main.js"></script>

The Module Script

This is the modern, chic way. Adding `type="module"` lets you use `import` and `export` directly in your browser-side JavaScript, just like in Node.js! It's essential for keeping your front-end code as organized as your closet.


<script type="module" src="/assets/js/main.js"></script>
