Tools & Techniques: Your Glam Squad

Every star needs a glam squad to make sure the final look is flawless. These are the essential tools and techniques that perfect your project behind the scenes.

Git: The Fashion Archive

Think of Git as your personal fashion archive. It's a version control system that lets you save snapshots of your project's code. If you mess up a look, you can just go back to a previous version. It's, like, a total lifesaver for preventing major fashion disasters.

Nodemon: The Quick Touch-up Artist

OMG, restarting your server manually every time you make a change is so boring. Nodemon is your personal touch-up artist. It watches your files, and the second you save a change, it automatically restarts your server for you. It's a non-negotiable for fast, fabulous development.

Environment Variables: Your Secret Notes

Some things are meant to be kept private, like your secret crush or your API keys. Environment variables are where you store this secret info. They live outside your code, so you never accidentally share them with the world. You can access them in Node.js with `process.env`.


// In your terminal, you might set a variable like this:
// PORT=8080 node server.js

// In your server.js file
const port = process.env.PORT || 3000; // Use the secret PORT, or 3000 if it's not there.

Clean Code: Flawless Presentation

This is the most important rule of all: your code has to look good. A messy, unorganized codebase is the ultimate fashion faux pas. Use clear variable names, keep your functions small, and add comments where things are tricky. A clean look is always in style.
